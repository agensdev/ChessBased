How to use ChessBased

ChessBased is an opening repertoire trainer. Practice your openings against a bot that plays statistically realistic responses from real Lichess games.

Quick Start

  1. Choose White or Black to start practicing.
  2. Play your moves on the board — the bot responds with popular moves from the database.
  3. Lock moves you want to learn by clicking the lock icon in the explorer panel.
  4. Start a New Game to practice your locked lines again.

Key Concepts

  • Locked moves — moves saved in your repertoire. The lock icon in the explorer marks moves as "must learn".
  • Openings — group locked moves into named openings. Use Browse or Import PGN to add lines.
  • Alerts — colored banners warn about critical positions. Click an alert to see the best move arrow.
  • Explorer — shows move statistics from millions of Lichess games. Click a move to play it.

Tips

  • Hover any explorer move to preview it as an arrow on the board.
  • Click alert banners to highlight the best move.
  • Green moves match your repertoire, red means you deviated.
  • Use Manual mode to explore freely without a bot opponent.
